About

Proteins interact with each other and organize themselves into macromolecular machines (ie. complexes) to carry out essential functions of the cell.

    Protein interactions
  • We derived protein interactions from co-fractionation mass spectrometry of 12 taxonomically diverse plant species
  • These are stable protein interactions conserved across plants
  • Interactions can be queried using either UniProt Entry, Entry name, or TAIR AT number.
  • False discovery rates of protein interactions above CF-MS score 0.5 are 10%, and above CF-MS score 0.2 are 50%
  • Interactions with CF-MS scores > 0.5 are descriptive of stable interactions, but a score above 0.2 is commonly descriptive of a more transient protein interactions
Protein complexes
  • Protein complexes were derived from interactions that pass high thresholds
  • Protein complexes are presented with four different levels of clustering stringency, from most restrictive to most inclusive
Orthology
  • As protein complexes were derived from multi-species data, they are indexed by eggNOG DB plant-level orthogroupID
  • These orthogroups take the form ENOG411XXXX, and can be searched at the eggNOG website
  • Due to gene expansions since the last common ancestor of plant, each orthogroup may contain more than one protein from a species
